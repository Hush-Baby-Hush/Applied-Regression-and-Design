Variable Selection

Example: State.x77 Data: matrix with 50 rows and 8 columns

  • Population: population estimate as of July 1, 1975
  • Income: per capita income (1974)
  • Illiteracy: illiteracy (1970, percent of population)
  • Life.Exp: life expectancy in years (196971)
  • Murder: murder and non-negligent manslaughter rate per 100,000 population (1976)
  • HS.Grad: percent high-school graduates (1970)
  • Frost: mean number of days with minimum temperature below freezing (19311960) in capital or large city
  • Area: land area in square miles

Variable selection with leaps package.

Aic = n*log(rs$rss/n) + 2*msize;
Bic = n*log(rs$rss/n) + msize*log(n);
plot(msize, Aic, xlab="No. of Parameters", ylab = "AIC");
plot(msize, Bic, xlab="No. of Parameters", ylab = "BIC");

Remark on R functions AIC and BIC: \(\textbf{R}\) has built in functions AIC and BIC for extracting these metrics from a model objects. In the penalty these functions use \(p+1\) instead of \(p\) due to estimating the residual variance \(\sigma^2\). This difference does not affect the minimization because it is simply another additive constant in the criterion.
